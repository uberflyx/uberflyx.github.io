Projects

  • 2018 - Present
    Research Grants Overview
    These are only my major project grants, and is not a comprehensive list.
  • 2024 - 2024
    Bytes over Lights: Levelling up community connectivity with cost-effective optical wireless communication
    (PI) This project addresses the digital divide in rapidly urbanizing developing nations, where conventional internet infrastructure is cost-prohibitive. Despite high mobile coverage in South Africa, affordability remains a significant barrier. The research aims to narrow this gap by integrating innovative optical wireless technology with existing community WiFi networks. Collaborating with RIOT Network and PeCo Power, the project will enhance network speed and reliability while ensuring consistent operation through solar power solutions, particularly crucial during frequent power outages. This approach seeks to provide more affordable and accessible internet connectivity in low-income urban areas.
    • Royal Academy of Engineering, Transforming Systems through Partnership (UK)
    • Partners: Martin Lavery (U of Glasgow), RIOT Network, PeCo Power
  • 2023 - 2025
    Levelling up wireless optical communications with structured light and machine learning
    (PI) This project aims to address the digital divide in Africa by developing affordable, long-range Free Space Optical (FSO) communication technology. The research focuses on increasing the range and bandwidth of FSO systems using innovative techniques such as modal diversity, mode division multiplexing, and machine learning. The goal is to create a prototype FSO system that provides multi-gigabit data throughput over long distances using cost-effective, off-the-shelf hardware, potentially bridging the last few kilometers to fiber networks in developing countries.
    • NRF Thuthuka (South Africa)
  • 2021 - 2023
    "Fibre" before the fibre: Bridging the Digital Divide in Informal Settlements
    (PI) This project addresses the critical need for communication infrastructure in rapidly growing informal settlements in developing nations like South Africa. The research focuses on implementing a prototype self-powered, multi-gigabit wireless optical communication system to provide fast and reliable internet access to a primary school in an underserved community. The project aims to deliver immediate educational benefits through international school connections and develop a technical roadmap for broader deployment. Key objectives include investigating the requirements, viability, and potential impact of easy-to-install, solar-powered wireless optical connectivity in peri-urban informal settlements, engaging with various stakeholders to create a strategy for rapid rollout of communication technologies in emerging communities internationally.
    • Royal Academy of Engineering, Transforming Systems through Partnership (UK)
    • Partners: Martin Lavery (U of Glasgow), Andrew Ellis (Aston), Andrew Forbes (Wits), PeCo Power
  • 2020 - 2023
    The Development of a Low-Cost, High-Speed and Long-Range Free-Space Optical Communication Transceiver
    (PI) This project addresses the digital divide in Africa by developing a prototype Free Space Optical (FSO) communication system. The research aims to create a reliable, long-range, multi-gigabit data throughput system using cost-effective, off-the-shelf hardware. The project explores innovative optical techniques such as modal diversity and mode division multiplexing, combined with optimized digital signal processing. The goal is to provide a viable alternative to current radio-based technologies, potentially retrofitting existing microwave link high-sites with FSO technology. This research not only aims to bridge the gap in internet infrastructure but also presents a significant business opportunity for a locally-manufactured product.
    • NRF Thuthuka (South Africa)
  • 2018 - 2019
    Miner Safety System: Tracking Underground Miners with their Headlamps
    (PI) This project aimed to develop an innovative Internet-of-Things (IoT) solution for improving miner safety and operational efficiency in underground mines. The system utilizes a combination of Power Line Communication (PLC) and Visible Light Communication (VLC) technologies to create a robust information transmission network. By modifying LED-based miners' headlamps to emit unique, imperceptible signatures and installing sensors throughout the mine, the system enables continuous location tracking of miners. Additional features include pedometers for movement monitoring and temperature sensors for environmental tracking. This comprehensive approach not only enhances rescue efforts in emergencies but also provides valuable data for optimizing mining operations, improving both safety and efficiency.
    • Technology Innovation Agency (South Africa)
    • Partners: Ling Cheng (Wits)
